Privacy

CurbTime Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2, 2026

CurbTime (“the app”) is built by an independent developer. This policy describes how information is handled when you use the app. If you have questions, contact us at the email listed at the end.

Summary

  • CurbTime does not require an account.
  • Your parking reminders and optional photos are stored on your device, not on our servers (we do not operate a backend that stores your reminder data).
  • We do not sell your personal information.

Information the app uses

Reminders and settings

Information you enter in the app (for example, deadlines, time windows, and optional notes tied to a reminder) is saved locally on your device using standard iOS storage. It is used only to provide the app’s features (countdowns, notifications, and Live Activities where supported).

Photos

If you choose to add a photo (for example, of a street sign or meter), that image is kept on your device with your reminder. We do not upload your photos to our servers.

Notifications

If you allow notifications, the operating system may show alerts based on the reminders you create. Notification scheduling uses Apple’s UserNotifications framework. You can change notification permissions anytime in Settings → CurbTime → Notifications.

Live Activities

If you enable Live Activities for CurbTime, countdown or status information may appear on your Lock Screen or Dynamic Island according to Apple’s Live Activity rules and your device settings. You can manage this in Settings → CurbTime → Live Activities.

Data sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. The app is not designed to share your reminder content with other users.

Apple may process certain data according to your device settings and Apple’s privacy policies.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when we do. Continued use of the app after changes means you accept the updated policy.
